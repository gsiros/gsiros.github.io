About

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Northeastern University, Boston. I'm interested in computer security with a focus on adversarial machine learning and distributed & decentralized systems.

I am a member of the NDS2 Lab and I am fortunate to be under the joint supervision of Alina Oprea and Cristina Nita-Rotaru.

Before my PhD studies, I was an undergraduate student at the Athens University of Economics and Business, where I was a member of the MMlab. I was mentored and worked under the supervision of George C. Polyzos.

Previously, I have also been a research intern working with Yigitcan Kaya at the University of Maryland, College Park under the supervision of Tudor Dumitra»ô.

Research

I work on Security focusing on Distributed & Decentralized Systems and Systems for Machine Learning. My research aims to explore the intersection of these fields, with a focus on building secure, decentralized systems that can effectively handle and protect sensitive data, while also enabling the use of advanced machine learning techniques. I am particularly interested in the design and implementation of secure communication protocols, access control mechanisms, and privacy-preserving techniques in decentralized systems.
