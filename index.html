About

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Northeastern University, Boston. I'm interested in computer security, with a focus on machine learning as a key element within a broader pipeline of components in software systems.

I am a member of the NDS2 Lab and I am fortunate to be under the joint supervision of Alina Oprea and Cristina Nita-Rotaru.

Before my PhD studies, I was an undergraduate student at the Athens University of Economics and Business, where I was a member of the MMlab. I was mentored and worked under the supervision of George C. Polyzos.

Previously, I have also been a research intern working with Yigitcan Kaya at the University of Maryland, College Park under the supervision of Tudor Dumitraș.

Research

My research focuses on security in distributed and decentralized systems, with an emphasis on machine learning as a component within broader system pipelines. I explore how machine learning can be securely integrated into complex software ecosystems, ensuring robustness, privacy, and resilience. My work spans secure machine learning for database management systems, federated learning security, and secure agentic systems, aiming to build security frameworks that protect sensitive data while maintaining system efficiency. I am particularly interested in designing privacy-preserving architectures, secure communication protocols, and access control mechanisms that enable machine learning to function safely within larger, interconnected systems.
